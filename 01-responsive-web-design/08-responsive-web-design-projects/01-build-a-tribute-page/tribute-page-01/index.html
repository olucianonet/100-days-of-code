Luis Gama Brazilian Abolitionist Hero

Life & Career

  • 1830: Luiz Gonzaga Pinto da Gama born on June 21, in Salvador, Bahia.
  • 1840: He becomes captive and is sold by his own father.
  • 1847: He begins his literacy process.
  • 1848: Knowing to read and write, it obtains irrefutable proofs of the illegality of its condition, since it was the son of a free woman.
  • 1848: Already free, serves as a soldier for 6 years.
  • 1849: He publishes his first work, Primeiras Trovas Burlescas, which placed him in the literary pantheon of Brazil only twelve years after he had learned to read.
  • 1850: He married and tried to attend the Law Course of Largo do São Francisco, because he was black, faced the hostility of teachers and students, but persisted as a listener. He did not finish the course, but the acquired knowledge allowed him to act in the legal defense of black slaves.
  • 1854: He leaves the corporation after being arrested for insubordination.
  • 1856: He is appointed clerk of the São Paulo Police Department.
  • 1860: From this decade on, he excelled as a journalist and collaborator of several progressive periodicals, besides his performance in the courts, where according to some estimates he was able to free approximately 1000 slaves. The causes were diverse, many involving blacks who could pay emancipation letters, but were prevented by their masters from being released, or who had entered national territory after the slave trade ban in 1850.
  • 1864: Together with caricaturist Angelo Agostini, he founded the capital's first humorous newspaper, entitled Diabo Coxo.
  • 1866: Still with Agostini, now with Américo de Campos adhesion, they founded the weekly newspaper Cabrião.
  • 1868: Participates in the foundation of Masonic America Lodge, along with Ruy Barbosa, Joaquim Nabuco, and others.
  • 1882: Luís Gama died on August 24, at the age of 52.
